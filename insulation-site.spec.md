This document defines the REQUIRED and OPTIONAL sections for a typical
residential insulation company website.

AI ASSISTANTS:
- Treat REQUIRED sections as mandatory
- Do not invent services that are not listed
- Use clear, homeowner-friendly language
- Avoid technical jargon unless explained
- Always prioritize lead generation and trust

---

## 1. SITE GOALS

Primary goals:
- Generate qualified estimate requests
- Build trust for a high-cost home service
- Educate homeowners enough to reduce bad leads
- Emphasize local service area

Secondary goals:
- Improve local SEO
- Reduce phone back-and-forth
- Set pricing expectations without quoting

---

## 2. CORE PAGES (REQUIRED)

### Homepage
Purpose:
- Immediately explain what the company does
- Show credibility
- Drive estimate requests

Must include:
- Clear headline stating insulation services
- Primary CTA: "Free Estimate"
- Phone number (click-to-call on mobile)
- Service area mention
- Short trust section (years in business, local focus)
- Link to estimate form

---

### Estimate / Contact Page
Purpose:
- Capture qualified leads

Required form fields:
- Name
- Phone
- Email
- Project type (attic, basement, crawlspace, whole home)
- Insulation type (spray foam, fiberglass, cellulose, unsure)
- Location (town or ZIP)

Optional:
- Square footage
- Preferred time window
- Message

After submit:
- Show confirmation message
- Trigger notification to business owner
- Send confirmation email to homeowner

---

## 3. SERVICES (REQUIRED)

Create individual service pages for each service offered.

Typical services:
- Attic insulation
- Basement insulation
- Crawlspace insulation
- Spray foam insulation
- Fiberglass insulation
- Cellulose insulation

Each service page MUST include:
- Problem the service solves
- Signs the homeowner needs this service
- Why this insulation type works
- What the installation involves
- Clear service-specific CTA

Do NOT include pricing quotes.

---

## 4. TRUST & CREDIBILITY (REQUIRED)

Include trust signals across the site.

Must include:
- Years in business
- Local service area
- Licensed / insured statement (if applicable)
- Certifications or affiliations (if available)

Social proof:
- Minimum 2 testimonials
- Before / after photos if available
- Avoid fake or stock testimonials

---

## 5. PROCESS TRANSPARENCY (REQUIRED)

Explain the installation process clearly.

Typical steps:
1. Inspect the home
2. Recommend insulation solution
3. Professional installation
4. Cleanup and walkthrough

Goal:
- Reduce fear and uncertainty
- Set expectations

---

## 6. EDUCATION & BUYER CONFIDENCE (REQUIRED)

Provide plain-English education.

Must include:
- Explanation of R-value
- Comparison of insulation types
- Common problems:
  - Cold rooms
  - Ice dams
  - High energy bills
- Guidance on choosing the right insulation

Tone:
- Helpful
- Non-salesy
- Easy to understand

---

## 7. PRICING GUIDANCE (OPTIONAL BUT RECOMMENDED)

Allowed:
- Factors that affect cost
- Typical project ranges (if provided by client)

Do NOT:
- Promise exact pricing
- Compete on cheapest price

Goal:
- Filter unrealistic leads
- Reduce price shock

---

## 8. LOCAL SEO (REQUIRED)

Include local relevance.

Must include:
- Service area language (towns, counties, region)
- Google Map embed
- Consistent business name, address, phone
- Location mentioned naturally in headings and copy

---

## 9. SCHEDULING (OPTIONAL / UPGRADE)

Supported scheduling approaches:
- Estimate request with preferred time window
- Online estimate booking (controlled availability)

Do NOT:
- Allow full job scheduling
- Expose internal calendars

If enabled:
- Send confirmation and reminder messages
- Reduce no-shows

---

## 10. AUTOMATION (OPTIONAL / UPGRADE)

Automation may include:
- Email notifications on form submit
- SMS alerts to owner
- Auto-reply confirmation to homeowner
- Lead logging (sheet, CRM, dashboard)

Automation should:
- Reduce response time
- Improve professionalism
- Require minimal client setup

---

## 11. SEASONAL CONTENT (OPTIONAL)

Recommended seasonal themes:
- Winter: heat loss, ice dams
- Summer: hot attics, cooling efficiency
- Fall: pre-winter prep

Use to:
- Improve SEO
- Keep site relevant year-round

---

## 12. QUALITY RULES

- Mobile-first layout
- Fast load times
- Clear CTAs on every major page
- No placeholder text at launch
- Avoid stock images when possible

---

## 13. DEFINITION OF DONE

The site is considered COMPLETE when:
- All REQUIRED sections exist
- Forms are tested end-to-end
- CTAs function correctly
- Content matches services actually offered
- Site is ready for client review or launch
